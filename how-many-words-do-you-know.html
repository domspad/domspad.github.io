How Many Words Do You Know?

Mon 04 April 2016 By Dominic Spadacene

Learning a new language makes everything exciting. In Budapest, I remember a day visiting the bakery as an amazing rush, just because the nonsense noises I made generated baked goods.

There is a point in your development when you get curious about LIVING the language. Then comes the daunting realization what that means.

Then, you realize you can express everything you want, but somehow, it still feels funny. There are words missing. One linguist attempted to categorize language knowledge in terms of the number of words known.

If it took you 3 times to see a word before you "knew" it, how many books would you have to read to achieve each level? In the top image, I tried it out for the series of books written by the Hungarian author Rejtő Jenő. Actually its quite surprising how quickly you get there until you realize that these are entire books.
